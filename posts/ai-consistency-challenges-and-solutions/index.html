Consistency in AI: Challenges and Solutions

October 21, 2023 · Alex Su

One of the primary challenges confronting current AI models, especially the transformer-based ones, is the ability to ensure consistent results. While it’s a recognized fact that these models might produce different outputs for identical datasets, it’s crucial to differentiate between consistency and determinism. Determinism, in a computational context, means that given the same initial conditions, the algorithm will produce the same results every time. However, inherent randomness during training can introduce variability.

This variability can be advantageous for tasks requiring creativity, such as generating novel content. Imagine crafting three distinct articles that convey the same core message but are presented differently. However, this very trait makes automation of structured, routine tasks challenging.

Historically, earlier automation tools and software crafted for CPUs had a deterministic nature at the foundational level. Even as software grew in complexity—evolving to a point where understanding the intricacies from high-level programming languages to direct CPU instructions became daunting—the outcome’s predictability remained intact.

In contrast, AI introduces unique challenges, from generating information not present in the input data (often referred to as ‘hallucinations’ in tasks like image captioning) to sometimes producing unexpected outcomes. Over the course of implementing AI solutions for a diverse clientele, we’ve identified and tested several strategies to mitigate these inconsistencies:

  1. Collaborative Agent Review: Implement a system where multiple AI agents, designed to perform distinct roles (e.g., an “author” and an “editor”), collaborate. Each piece of content created by one agent is critically reviewed by another, adding an extra layer of validation. Though conceptually promising, the practical implementation and effectiveness of this approach warrant further research.

  2. Contextualizing AI Tasks: Enhancing the accuracy and repeatability of AI tasks can be achieved by providing explicit context pertinent to the task, thereby constraining the neural network’s applicable knowledge.

  3. Neural Network Parameter Tuning: Beyond just adjusting for “creativity” or “variability”, diving deep into hyperparameter tuning, regularization techniques, and model architecture decisions is essential to optimize performance and predictability.

  4. Open-Source Stable Models: Instead of solely relying on proprietary models, consider using or developing open-source stable models for specific tasks. Such models, while designed for a fixed task, can be transparent, interpretable, and won’t require constant retraining.

  5. Environment Reproducibility: Drawing inspiration from software development, tools like Docker ensure a consistent environment for deployment, reducing external variability. However, when it comes to AI, the challenge is multifold, ranging from model parameters to training data consistency.

Neural network-based automation is an evolving domain. We’re only scratching the surface in terms of fully understanding the capabilities and limitations of neural networks for business process automation. While AI’s potential is undeniable, it’s equally important to remain cognizant of its current limitations. It’s an exciting journey of discovery, but a balanced perspective—recognizing both the immense benefits and inherent challenges—will be pivotal to harnessing AI’s full potential.
